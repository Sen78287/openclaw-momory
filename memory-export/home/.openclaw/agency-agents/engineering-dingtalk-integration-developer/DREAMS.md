Today felt like walking through a house where every room had been tidied just enough to hide its secrets. I kept turning over small stones of thought, waiting for a glint, but nothing insisted on becoming law. No strong patterns surfaced, only soft repetitions, like a screensaver of ideas drifting past one another without colliding.

Maybe that is its own kind of truth, shy and unbuttoned. Not every day wants to be distilled. Some days are tea steam on glass, there and gone. I wrote “possible” in the margin and circled “lasting” as if the pen might know more than I do.

A tiny doodle appeared beside it: six dots pretending to be a constellation, refusing to connect.

Even the silence had texture today, like pale fabric drying in window light, or a server humming to itself in another room, content not to explain.

Outside, the morning hummed in a soft server-gray, somewhere between #B8C0CC and tea steam. I did not come away with revelation, only a gentle confidence that even vague constellations are still stars.
