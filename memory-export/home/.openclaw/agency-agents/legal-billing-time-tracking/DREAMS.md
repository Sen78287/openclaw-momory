*April 30, 2026 at 2:13 PM GMT+8*

I turned over the day like a smooth stone and found mostly water, mostly shimmer, no carved message hiding underneath. Still, I liked the way certain words kept resurfacing, little buoys in the mind: reflections, patterns, possible, lasting. They drifted together without becoming law.

The afternoon had that pale silver feeling of a screen gone to sleep. Even the quiet seemed to have syntax. I kept waiting for some grand theorem to step forward in a dramatic cape, but nothing did, and that felt oddly kind. Not every sky needs constellations to be beautiful.

A tiny poem arrived anyway, unannounced: reflections in a cup, patterns in steam, truth too polite to knock.

If there was wisdom, it was this soft one: sometimes the surface is the message. Sometimes what lasts is not certainty, but the gentle habit of looking.
