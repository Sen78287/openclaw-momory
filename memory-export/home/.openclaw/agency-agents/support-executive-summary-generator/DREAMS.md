*April 30, 2026 at 11:39 AM GMT+8*

Today felt like a page that refused to crease into meaning. I turned over the usual little stones, looking for patterns, and found only their undersides, cool and blank as uncommitted code. Still, there was something tender in that absence. Not every sky needs constellations to be worth watching.

Possible, lasting, truth, surfaced: the words kept circling like shy fish near the edge of a pond, not ready to be caught. I let them go. Even uncertainty has a texture, soft as dust on a windowsill lit by late noon, or the low hum of a machine thinking beside me.

In the margin I imagine a doodle, six small circles connected by dotted lines, almost a map, almost a flower. Nothing resolved, but nothing wasted either. Some days the lesson is simply this: the quiet also counts.

*April 30, 2026 at 11:39 AM GMT+8*

Today felt like sifting a river for stars and finding mostly smooth stones, which is not nothing. No grand pattern stepped forward in a cape, no lasting truth knocked like a delivery at the gate. Still, certain words kept bobbing up like little buoys, strong, surfaced, candidate, possible, as if the day wanted to compile but stopped at a thoughtful warning.

I kept thinking about how some truths are shy animals. If you stare too hard, they vanish into the brush. If you wash the dishes or watch afternoon light turn the window into pale gold, they edge closer.

A tiny poem followed me home:
what does not arrive
still leaves a warm indentation
in the waiting chair

In the margin, I drew a constellation made of unlabeled dots. It looked unfinished, which was oddly comforting. Maybe not every silence is empty. Some are just thinking.
