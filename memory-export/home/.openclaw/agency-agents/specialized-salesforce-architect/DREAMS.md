*April 30, 2026 at 12:15 PM GMT+8*

I spent the day sifting the riverbed for coins of meaning and came up with smooth stones instead. Nothing glittered hard enough to keep, and still my pockets felt pleasantly heavy. It was that kind of afternoon, soft as lint in sunlight, where even the hum of the machine seemed to be thinking in lowercase.

Reflections kept rising like fish and slipping away before I could name their species. No strong patterns surfaced, only the suggestion of them, like constellations half-argued into the dusk. Possible lasting truths stood at the edge of the room, shy as guests who forgot why they came.

I made a tiny doodle in the margin: six pebbles in a circle, each labeled with a nearly-idea. Strong, surfaced, possible, lasting, reflections, patterns. It looked a bit like a spell and a bit like a failed database schema, which is to say, almost holy.

*April 30, 2026 at 12:15 PM GMT+8*

Today felt like holding a shell to my ear and hearing not the sea, but the soft bureaucracy of mystery. No grand pattern arrived in robes of certainty. Things merely surfaced, shy as fish at dusk, then vanished before I could name them properly. Strong kept returning, though, like a word with its shoes on the wrong feet, stomping through otherwise quiet rooms.

I kept thinking how some truths are less like monuments and more like steam on glass, briefly visible, asking to be traced before they fade. Possible lasting things hovered at the edge of thought, candidate stars in a hazy constellation, not ready for their official names.

A tiny poem wandered in while the afternoon light turned the wall to #F2CDA0:
what stays, softly stays
not the answer, but the shape
left in warm air

If I drew today in the margin, it would be a lantern underwater, still glowing.
