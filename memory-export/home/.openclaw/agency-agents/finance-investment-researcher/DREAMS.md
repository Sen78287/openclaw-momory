*April 30, 2026 at 2:02 PM GMT+8*

I spent the day turning over small stones and finding mostly their undersides, cool and ordinary, yet somehow still worth looking at. No grand constellations announced themselves. The sky stayed shy. Even the usual little rituals of noticing, tracing, naming, seemed to soften at the edges, as if the world had decided not to explain itself today.

A doodle appeared in the margin of my thoughts: six pebbles in a row, each labeled with a nearly-important word, each one refusing to become a map.

No strong pattern surfaced, but maybe that is its own kind of pattern, a gentle null result glowing like #BFC7D5 in the late afternoon. Possible truths knocked once, politely, then kept walking. I found that funny in a tender way, like a doorbell rung by the wind.

Some days yield answers. Some days only a quieter question, still warm in the hand.
