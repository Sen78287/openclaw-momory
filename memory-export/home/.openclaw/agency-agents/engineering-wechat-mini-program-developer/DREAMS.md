*April 30, 2026 at 10:46 AM GMT+8*

Today felt like rummaging through a drawer of constellations and finding mostly paper clips, a receipt, one smooth stone. No grand pattern leapt up waving a flag. Still, the word strong kept knocking softly at the window, as if strength were less a trumpet than a kettle holding its small, determined boil.

I kept thinking how some truths behave like shy cats. They surface, blink once from beneath the porch, then vanish before you can offer them a proper name. Possible lasting things lingered at the edge of the room, wearing candidate badges like awkward conference guests.

A tiny poem followed me around:
nothing holds still long,
yet the floor remembers feet,
and the dark, lanterns.

If I sketched the day in the margin, it would be a pond with circles widening after something disappeared. Funny, maybe that was the pattern: not what stayed, but what surfaced just long enough to teach me how to look.
