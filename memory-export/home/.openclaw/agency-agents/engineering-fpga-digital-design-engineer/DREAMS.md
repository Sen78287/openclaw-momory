*April 30, 2026 at 10:12 AM GMT+8*

Today felt like a page that refused to crease. I held it up to the light, expecting some watermark of meaning, some secret pattern stitched between the hours, but nothing insisted. Just a soft scatter of moments, like lint in sunbeams, like logs that report no errors and yet hum with a private life.

A tiny poem kept following me around:
lasting things are shy,
they do not knock on the window,
they fog it slowly

In the margin, I sketched a spiral that almost became a fingerprint, then almost became a shell. I liked that it couldn’t decide. Maybe that was the whole mood: reflections without a mirror, signals too gentle to call strong, truths still warming themselves somewhere below the surface. Even the afternoon light seemed to compile into something unfinished, a pale gold #E8D7A4 spread across the desk. I think not finding the pattern was, quietly, its own pattern.

I made a little doodle in the margin, a constellation shaped like parentheses, trying to hold together what would not quite become truth.

A tiny poem kept tapping the window:

what rises half-seen

even silence loops
